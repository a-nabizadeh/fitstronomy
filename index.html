WHAT I OFFER

Services

All programs are continuously updated to reflect the newest findings in sports science, so you always train smarter, not just harder.

Strength Training

Build muscle and improve power with structured, periodized programs designed around your body and goals.

SCIENCE BEHIND THIS
RESEARCH FACT

Adults who do not strength train lose up to 5 lbs of muscle per decade. Progressive resistance training reverses this, increasing muscle protein synthesis by up to 50% and improving metabolic rate, bone density, and insulin sensitivity simultaneously.

Source: Westcott, W.L., Current Sports Medicine Reports, 2012

Weight Loss

Sustainable fat loss through smart nutrition strategies and progressive cardio, with no crash diets and no shortcuts.

SCIENCE BEHIND THIS
RESEARCH FACT

Combining resistance training with a moderate calorie deficit preserves lean mass during fat loss far better than cardio alone. Studies show muscle-preserving deficits of 300–500 kcal per day produce superior long-term body composition outcomes compared to aggressive cuts.

Source: Barakat et al., Strength & Conditioning Journal, 2020

Online Coaching

Remote programs and regular check-ins that fit your schedule, wherever you are in the world.

SCIENCE BEHIND THIS
RESEARCH FACT

Accountability and structured programming are the two strongest predictors of long-term exercise adherence. Research shows that coached individuals, including remotely, are 78% more likely to maintain consistent training habits over 6 months than those training without guidance.

Source: Olander et al., Journal of Behavioral Medicine, 2013

In-Person Sessions

One-on-one training with hands-on coaching, real-time form correction, and immediate feedback.

SCIENCE BEHIND THIS
RESEARCH FACT

Real-time coaching feedback reduces injury risk and accelerates motor learning. Studies comparing supervised versus unsupervised training found that supervised participants achieved significantly greater strength gains in 12 weeks, with measurably better movement quality and exercise technique.

Source: Coutts et al., Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research, 2004
